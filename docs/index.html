SemIoE Ontology Specification Draft

Abstract

SemIoE is an OWL2 ontology designed to represent agents, smart devices, and various information entities within the Internet of Everything (IoE) ecosystem. This ontology aims to provide a structured and standardized way to describe entities and their relationships, fostering interoperability and enhancing the semantic understanding of IoE environments.

Introduction back to ToC

Recently, the Industry 5.0 is gaining attention as a novel paradigm, defining the next concrete steps toward more and more intelligent, green-aware and user-centric digital systems. In an era in which smart devices typically adopted in the industry domain are more and more sophisticated and autonomous, the Internet of Things and its evolution known as the Internet of Everything (IoE, for short), involving also people, robots, processes and data in the network, represent the main driver to allow industries to put the experiences and needs of human beings at the center of their ecosystems. However, due to the extreme heterogeneity of the involved entities, their intrinsic need and capability to cooperate, and the objective to decline a dynamic user-centric context, the integration and processing of the data produced by such an IoE require special attention. This is the objective of the SemIoE ontology, a novel semantic model that formalizes the fundamental agents, elements and information of an IoE, along with their relationships. In particular, relevant information that is represented in the ontology is categorized under several perspectives:
  • the smart objects perspective focuses on smart devices including one or more systems. In turn, each of it can be composed of a number of other devices such as sensors and actuators. Their technical capabilities, operating ranges, configurations and locations are described;
  • the environment perspective, which focuses on the enterprise environment and its structure in sub-components, e.g. buildings, floors, rooms;
  • the organization perspective, which includes the organizational structure in terms of sub-organizations and units, and the reporting structure for the employees, where they are located and what roles are defined for them;
  • the process perspective, which details processes in terms of activities in which employees are engaged.
Furthermore, two relevant perspectives can be identified that are orthogonal to the mentioned ones, namely:
  • preferences of employees related to environmental or smart devices' parameters;
  • access control, which focuses on what rights are associated with each organization role. Rights can be defined of three types with decreasing granularity: on environment, which apply on all systems located in the same place, on smart objects, which apply to all its sub-systems, and on specific systems. Furthermore, rights can be transferred among employees or smart objects (hereby we refer to the term ``agent'' to consider both) in the execution of an activity/process, through different types of relations, namely delegation or collaboration. In the former case, the existing rights of the delegated agent for the delegated activity are replaced with those of the delegating agent. Delegation can happen only if the delegated agent is not involved in another activity. On the other hand, in the latter case, the rights already owned by the agent are combined with those of the collaborating one.
In our design, we focus on state-of-the-art design principles, in particular reuse, and abstraction, to build a lightweight ontology inheriting and extending concepts from well-known and consolidated reference ontologies. Following widely adopted best practices on reusing ontological models, indeed the development of the SemIOE ontology starts from the identification of relevant existing vocabularies/ontologies focusing on specific aspects of the IoE:
  • Semantic Sensor Network (SSN)[SSN] and Sensor, Observation, Sample, and Actuator (SOSA) [SOSA] ontologies for the representation of sensors, actuators and related platforms
  • Building Topology Ontology [BOT] for the representation of environments
  • Org ontology [ORG] for the representation of organization units, their roles and structure.
These ontological modules have been integrated through a bridge ontology which is graphically represented in Figure 1. On the one hand, it allows to define the needed connections and alignments between relevant classes of different ontologies for the realization of a comprehensive model of the IoE environment. On the other hand, the bridge ontology defines further classes and properties to represent concepts that are relevant for the IoE environment and are not available in external ontologies.
